Transparency Statement

Last Updated: 24 January 2026

At MBT, transparency is one of our core principles. We want every user to understand how our service operates, what it costs to run, and why certain features may require a small subscription fee. Our goal is simple: keep MBT accessible to everyone while ensuring the platform can sustainably operate long-term.

Our Commitment

MBT will always be free to use. Any feature that is currently free will remain free permanently. We will never place existing free features behind a paywall.

Paid features are introduced only when a feature has an ongoing cost to operate. If we can offer something at no cost, we will.

Keeping Costs Low

To keep subscription prices and operational costs as low as possible, MBT self-hosts the majority of its infrastructure. This avoids expensive third-party service fees and allows us to run the platform far more efficiently than typical commercial solutions.

Whenever a component can be self-hosted safely and reliably, we choose that option. This is the primary reason MBT can operate sustainably while keeping user costs minimal.

Why Some Features Are Paid

The only paid features are those that require significant server resources or recurring costs. Snap-to-Road is the primary example. If MBT relied on an external routing provider, the expected cost would be £400–£700 per month.

By self-hosting, this cost is reduced to £40–£50 per month, allowing us to keep pricing low.
